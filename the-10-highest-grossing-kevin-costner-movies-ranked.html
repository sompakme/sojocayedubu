The 10 Highest-Grossing Kevin Costner Movies, Ranked

September 2024 · 6 minute read

  • No Way Out - 1987 ($84.3 million)
  • Field of Dreams - 1989 ($84.4 million)

  • JFK - 1991 ($205 million)
  • The Bodyguard - 1992 ($410 million)
  • Dances with Wolves - 1990 ($424 million)

As a fan of Hollywood, you know that some actors are just able to consistently deliver captivating performances, no matter the genre. Kevin Costner is certainly one of those actors. With a career spanning decades, he has become a household name and a true icon of the film industry. In this list, we've compiled the top 10 highest-grossing movies featuring Kevin Costner. From heart-wrenching dramas to action-packed thrillers, these films showcase the range and depth of this talented actor's abilities. are you ready to see which of Kevin Costner's movies have made the biggest splash at the box office? Let's get started!

The War - 1994 ($75.7 million)

Via Flickr/JustWatch

Jon Avnet is the director and producer of the drama The War, which tells the story of a young boy named Willie struggling to deal with the effects of the Vietnam War on his family. Kevin Costner played the role of Vietnam veteran Lowell Coleman in the film and was joined by fellow actors Elijah Wood, Lexi Randall, and Mare Winningham. The War grossed $75.7 million at the box office which was about the best it could have grossed at that time.

No Way Out - 1987 ($84.3 million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Directed by Roger Donaldson and produced by Arnold Kopelson, the political thriller No Way Out was released in 1987. The movie is about a naval officer, played by Kevin Costner, who becomes involved in a complex and dangerous conspiracy. Costner was joined in the film by actors Gene Hackman, Will Patton, and Sean Young. No Way Out grossed $84.3 million at the box office.

Field of Dreams - 1989 ($84.4 million)

Via Flickr/Movie Rewind

Released in 1989, Field of Dreams is a drama about the power of forgiveness and redemption. The film was directed by Phil Alden Robinson and produced by Lawrence Gordon and Charles Gordon. Kevin Costner starred in the film as Ray Kinsella, a farmer who builds a baseball diamond in his cornfield after hearing a mysterious voice. The movie also featured actors such as James Earl Jones, Amy Madigan, and Burt Lancaster. Field of Dreams grossed $84.4 million at the box office.

Tin Cup - 1996 ($105 million)

Via Flickr/Golf Digest

In 1996, Ron Shelton directed and produced the sports comedy Tin Cup, which tells the story of a down-on-his-luck golf pro named Roy “Tin Cup" McAvoy. Kevin Costner played the lead role in the film and was joined by actors Rene Russo, Don Johnson, and Cheech Marin. Tin Cup follows McAvoy's journey as he tries to qualify for the U.S. Open and win back the love of his life. The movie grossed $105 million at the box office.

The Untouchables - 1987 ($106.2 million)

Via Flickr/IEVENN

Released in 1987, The Untouchables is a crime drama that tells the story of a group of law enforcement officers who are determined to bring down the infamous gangster, Al Capone. The film was directed by Brian De Palma and produced by Art Linson and Mario Puzo. Kevin Costner played the lead role of Eliot Ness, a federal agent who assembles a team to take down Capone. The film also featured actors such as Sean Connery, Robert De Niro, and Andy Garcia. The Untouchables grossed $106.2 million at the box office.

Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves - 1991 ($165.5 million)

Via Flickr/The Film Experience

Released in 1991, Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves is an action-adventure film that is based on the traditional story of Robin Hood. The film was directed by Kevin Reynolds and produced by Pen Densham, John Watson, and Paul Hitchcock. Kevin Costner starred in the film as Robin Hood, the legendary outlaw who fights against injustice in medieval England. The film also featured actors such as Morgan Freeman, Christian Slater, and Alan Rickman. Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves grossed $165.5 million at the box office.

The Postman - 1997 ($177.7 million)

Via Flickr/bishwabarta

In 1997, Kevin Costner directed and produced the post-apocalyptic drama The Postman. The film is set in a future where the United States has been devastated by a global conflict and follows the journey of a wanderer who becomes the unlikely leader of a group of survivors. Costner played the lead role in the film and was joined by actors Will Paton, Larenz Tate, and Olivia Williams. The Postman grossed $177.7 million at the box office.

JFK - 1991 ($205 million)

Via Flickr/The Poster Collector

Released in 1991, JFK is a political thriller that tells the story of the investigation into the assassination of President John F. Kennedy. The film was directed by Oliver Stone and produced by Arnon Milchan, A. Kitman Ho, and Stone. Kevin Costner played the lead role of Jim Garrison, the District Attorney of Orleans Parish who tries to uncover the truth about the assassination. JFK grossed $205 million at the box office.

The Bodyguard - 1992 ($410 million)

Via Flickr/Catchplay

The Bodyguard is a romantic thriller that tells the story of a former Secret Service agent who is hired to protect a famous singer from a stalker. The film was directed by Mick Jackson and produced by Lawrence Kasdan and Arnold Kopelson. Kevin Costner played the lead role of Frank Farmer, the bodyguard, and Whitney Houston played the role of Rachel Marron, the singer. The film also featured actors such as Gary Kemp, Bill Cobbs, and Ralph Waite and grossed $410 million at the box office.

Dances with Wolves - 1990 ($424 million)

Via Flickr/Michael R. Ritt

Dances with Wolves took the world by storm with its epic tale of friendship and understanding set against the backdrop of the American West. Directed and produced by Kevin Costner, who also starred as the film's protagonist, Lieutenant John Dunbar, the movie tells the story of a Union Army officer who befriends a tribe of Lakota Sioux Indians. Dances with Wolves won numerous awards and grossed a staggering $424 million at the box office.
