Early Life and Education

Linda Kozlowski, born on January 7, 1958, in Fairfield, Connecticut, had a fascinating journey that shaped her into the accomplished actress she is today. Growing up in a Polish-American family, Kozlowski’s early years were influenced by her cultural background and upbringing.

She attended Andrew Warde High School, where her passion for performing arts began to take shape. It was during this time that she discovered her love for acting and decided to pursue it as a career.

After graduating from high school in 1976, Kozlowski set her sights on honing her craft and refining her acting skills. She gained acceptance into the prestigious Juilliard School, a renowned performing arts conservatory in New York City. The opportunity to study at Juilliard was a pivotal moment in her life, as it allowed her to receive expert training from accomplished industry professionals.

In 1981, Kozlowski graduated from the drama division of Juilliard, equipped with the skills and knowledge needed for a successful career in the competitive world of entertainment. Her time at Juilliard not only provided her with a solid foundation in acting, but it also exposed her to a diverse range of theatrical experiences and collaborations.

With her exceptional education and talent, Linda Kozlowski was ready to embark on her journey in the entertainment industry, captivating audiences with her performances and leaving a lasting impact on the world of film and television.

Career Highlights

Linda Kozlowski’s career skyrocketed in 1986 when she landed the female lead role in the critically acclaimed Australian film “Crocodile Dundee.” This role not only brought her immense popularity but also earned her recognition for her acting abilities. Kozlowski’s portrayal of Sue Charlton, a journalist who falls in love with the adventurous Mick Dundee, captivated audiences worldwide.

“I think I’ve always been attracted to characters who are positive and come from a very innocent place. I think there’s a lot of room for discovery in these characters and that’s something I always value in people I want to hang out with – which probably makes me sound really boring.” – Linda Kozlowski

Kozlowski reprised her role as Sue Charlton in the film’s sequels, “Crocodile Dundee II” and “Crocodile Dundee in Los Angeles.” These sequels further showcased her talent and solidified her status as a Hollywood actress. While the “Crocodile Dundee” series remains the highlight of her career, Kozlowski’s filmography extends beyond this franchise.

She has appeared in several other notable films, including “Death of a Salesman,” “Pass the Ammo,” “Almost an Angel,” “Backstreet Justice,” “The Neighbour,” and “Village of the Damned.” These diverse roles allowed Kozlowski to demonstrate her versatility as an actress and engage with different genres.
