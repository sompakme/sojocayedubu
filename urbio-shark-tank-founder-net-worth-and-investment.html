Urbio Shark Tank Founder, Net Worth, and Investment

August 2024 · 12 minute read

Urbio is an indoor plant company that specializes in modern vertical gardens for those living in apartments and buildings without access to outdoor garden space. The company was founded by Jared Aller and Beau Oyler, two serial entrepreneurs and designers with a passion for home decor. During their appearance on Shark Tank in Season 4, Episode 13, the founders pitched their idea and secured a deal with Lori Greiner. They asked for $300,000 in exchange for 12% of equity and ultimately accepted an offer of $300,000 for 15% of equity. Since their appearance on the show, Urbio has experienced significant growth, with a current net worth that remains undisclosed.

The Founders of Urbio

Urbio was founded by Jared Aller and Beau Oyler, two friends with a shared passion for entrepreneurship and design. Jared, the founder of design business Enlisted Design, brought his expertise in innovative product development, while Beau’s background in decorative home decor added a touch of style to the mix. Combining their entrepreneurial spirit and love for gardening, Jared and Beau embarked on a mission to bring nature indoors through the creation of Urbio.

Driven by their vision, the founders launched a successful Kickstarter campaign to fund their venture. With overwhelming support from the Kickstarter community, they not only surpassed their funding goal but also received widespread recognition for their innovative vertical gardens. This initial success propelled them to the next level and caught the attention of the Shark Tank producers.

Their appearance on Shark Tank showcased their entrepreneurial drive and the unique value proposition of Urbio. The founders impressed the Sharks with their innovative designs and vision for bringing greenery into small spaces. Their charismatic pitch resonated with the Sharks, leading to multiple offers.

“Urbio is more than just a business for us; it’s a way to bring nature and design together, creating harmony in people’s living spaces. We believe that everyone, regardless of the size of their home, should have the opportunity to experience the beauty of indoor gardening.”

Jared and Beau’s passion for their product and their genuine connection to the mission of Urbio ultimately attracted the attention and investment of Lori Greiner. They accepted Lori’s offer of $300,000 for 15% equity, securing the deal that would fuel the growth and success of Urbio.

Urbio’s Success and Growth

Since their appearance on Shark Tank, Urbio has experienced remarkable success and substantial growth in the plant-based home decorative industry. With a strong emphasis on design and customer satisfaction, their products have gained popularity among individuals looking to bring greenery and style into their living spaces.

Following their appearance on the show, Urbio’s sales soared, contributing to their overall valuation. Within a brief period of 1.5 to 2 months after their Shark Tank debut, Urbio received orders totaling an impressive $125,000. This surge in sales clearly resonated with consumers, highlighting the demand for their unique vertical gardens.

Recognizing the need to expand their reach, Urbio strategically increased their distribution network. They successfully tapped into thousands of retail outlets across the country, ensuring easy access for customers who wanted to incorporate Urbio’s products into their homes or offices. This expansion further drove the growth of the brand and solidified their position in the industry as a prominent player.

Capitalizing on their momentum, Urbio made a strategic move in 2015 by acquiring Sebi Space, a company specializing in manufacturing and designing accessories for bathroom and kitchen areas. This expansion into new sectors allowed Urbio to diversify their product range and cater to a wider audience with a variety of home organization solutions.

The compelling combination of innovative designs, expanding distribution channels, and product diversification has propelled Urbio to the forefront of the plant-based home decorative market. While specific revenue and valuation figures remain undisclosed, Urbio’s success story speaks for itself, as they continue to flourish and establish themselves as a trusted brand within the industry.

Urbio’s Acquisition and Current Status

In 2016, Urbio embarked on a new chapter when it was acquired by Honey-Can-Do International, a global supplier of storage and organization products. This strategic acquisition opened up new avenues for Urbio, expanding its reach and resources within the industry.

As of now, Urbio continues to thrive in the plant-based home decorative market. The acquisition has allowed them to further enhance their product offerings and maintain their commitment to quality, design, and customer satisfaction.

With its stylish and functional wall organizers, planters, and storage solutions, Urbio remains a top choice among individuals seeking to elevate their living spaces. Urbio’s current status reflects its influence and success in the industry, solidifying its position as a leading brand.
